Mr. Anka of Lincoln (for himself, Mr. Sun) introduced the following bill
A BILL
To establish an office within the State Department tasked with coordinating with nations,
international organizations, and merchants on safety in the Strait of Hormuz, ensuring
regional communication channels comply with U.S. law, and to provide necessary funding
and oversight mechanisms.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of
America in Congress assembled,
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
SHORT TITLE—This Act may be cited as the "Strait of Hormuz Security
Coordination Act of 2024.”
SEC. 2. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE OFFICE OF STRAIT OF HORMUZ
SECURITY COORDINATION
(a) ESTABLISHMENT—There is established within the Department of State an office
to be known as the “Office of Strait of Hormuz Security Coordination" (hereafter
referred to as "the Office").
(b) FUNCTIONS—The Office shall be responsible for:
i. Coordinating with nations bordering the Strait of Hormuz, international
organizations, and merchant entities to enhance safety and security in the
Strait of Hormuz.

ii. Opening and maintaining regional channels of communication to ensure
compliance with U.S. laws and international maritime regulations.
iii. Assessing and addressing threats to navigation and trade in the Strait of
Hormuz.
iv. Implementing measures to enhance maritime security, including but not
limited to safety monitoring and inspection protocols.
v. Imposing heightened safety monitoring and inspection fees associated with
trade tariffs for non-compliance by merchants and nations.
SEC. 3. FUNDING
(a) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS—-There are authorized to be
appropriated to the Department of State such sums as may be necessary to carry out
the activities of the Office for each of the fiscal month periods 2024 through 2029.
(b) AVAILABILITY OF FUNDS—Funds appropriated pursuant to the authorization
of appropriations in subsection (a) shall remain available until expended
SEC. 4. ANNUAL TESTIMONY
(a) TESTIMONY REQUIREMENT—-The Secretary of State shall testify annually
before the Senate Committee on Relations on the state of safety and security in the
Strait of Hormuz. This testimony shall include:
i. A detailed report on the activities and progress of the Office.
ii. An assessment of the current security situation in the Strait of Hormuz.
iii. Recommendations for legislative or policy changes to enhance maritime
safety and security in the region.
(b) INITIAL TESTIMONY—The initial testimony as required by subsection (a) shall
be provided no later than one year after the date of enactment of this Act.
SEC. 5. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT
(a) REGULATIONS—The Secretary of State shall promulgate all regulations
necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act, including the imposition of safety
monitoring and inspection fees.
(b) NON-COMPLIANCE CONSEQUENCES—Merchants and nations that fail to
comply with established safety protocols and communication channels as determined
by the Office shall be subject to:
i. Heightened safety monitoring and inspection fees in compliance with U.S.
law.
ii. Sanctions imposed under and as defined by US law.
SEC. 6. EFFECTIVE DATE
EFFECTIVE DATE—This Act shall take effect 90 days after the date of enactment.

SEC. 7. SEVERABILITY
SEVERABILITY—If any provision of this Act, or the application thereof, is held
invalid, the remainder of this Act and the application of such provision to other
persons or circumstances shall not be affected thereby.
SEC. 8. SUNSET PROVISION
This Act shall expire when the President of the United States has deemed the
situation in the Straits of Hormuz to no longer require the provisions of this act.
