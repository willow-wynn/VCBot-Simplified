Mr. Anka of Lincoln (for himself) introduced the following bill
A BILL
To amend the Endangered Species Act of 1973 (16 U.S.C. 1535) in order to enhance
conservation efforts through mandated minimum funding levels for both animal and plant
species.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of
America in Congress assembled,
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
SHORT TITLE—This Act may be cited as the "Endangered Species
Conservation Funding Enhancement Act of 2024.”
SECTION 1. FINDINGS
(a) FINDINGS—Congress finds the following:
i. The Endangered Species Act of 1973 has been instrumental in protecting
and recovering endangered and threatened species in the United States.
ii. Despite the Act's successes, many endangered plant and animal species
currently receive inadequate funding or no funding at all, hindering effective
conservation, ecosystem, and biodiversity protection efforts.

SEC. 2. AMENDMENTS TO THE ENDANGERED SPECIES ACT
(a) FUNDING MANDATE—Section 6 of the Endangered Species Act of 1973 (16
U.S.C. 1535) is amended to include the following provisions:
i. The Secretary of the Interior, in consultation with the Secretary of
Agriculture, shall allocate a minimum of $1,500 annually for each
endangered animal species listed under the Endangered Species Act for
conservation efforts.
ii. The Secretary of the Interior, in consultation with the Secretary of
Agriculture, shall allocate a minimum of $500 annually for each endangered
plant species listed under the Endangered Species Act for conservation
efforts.
(b) ALLOCATION CRITERIA–The allocation of funds shall prioritize projects and
initiatives aimed at conservation, habitat restoration, research, monitoring, and
public awareness campaigns for each endangered species.
SEC. 3. IMPLEMENTATION
(a) ENFORCEMENT—-The Secretary of the Interior shall establish guidelines and
procedures to ensure compliance with the funding mandates specified in Section
2(a).
(b) REPORTING—Each federal agency responsible for administering funds under
this Act shall submit an annual report to Congress detailing the allocation and
expenditure of funds for endangered species conservation efforts.
SEC. 4. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS
(a) APPROPRIATIONS—-There is authorized to be appropriated such sums as may
be necessary to carry out the provisions of this Act.
SEC. 5. EFFECTIVE DATE
(a) EFFECTIVE DATE—This Act shall take effect 90 days after its enactment into
law.
SEC. 7. SEVERABILITY
SEVERABILITY—If any provision of this Act, or the application thereof, is held
invalid, the remainder of this Act and the application of such provision to other
persons or circumstances shall not be affected thereby.
