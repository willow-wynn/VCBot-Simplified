S. 16
To promote and incentivize volunteerism by fostering
partnerships between community organizations, businesses,
and educational institutions.
IN THE SENATE
July 30, 2023
Mr. Guy (for himself, ) introduced the following bill, on behalf
of the Rosen Administration, as authored by President Rosen;
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
United States of America in Congress assembled,
A BILL

1. SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
This Act may be cited as the "Community Service Incentive Act
of 2023".
2. SEC. 2. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE.
2.1. FINDINGS.—
Congress finds the following:
(1) Volunteerism provides significant benefits to
communities, individuals, and the nation as a whole.
(2) Collaboration between community organizations,
businesses, and educational institutions is key to creating
meaningful volunteer opportunities and fostering a
culture of service.
2.2. PURPOSE.—
The purpose of this Act is to incentivize volunteerism by
establishing a national framework that promotes partnerships
between community organizations, businesses, and educational
institutions.
3. SEC. 3. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE VOLUNTEER
INCENTIVE PROGRAM.
3.1. IN GENERAL.—
The Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services
shall establish and oversee a program, to be known as the
Volunteer Incentive Program (VIP).
3.2. GRANTS. —
Under the VIP, the Secretary shall award grants on a competitive
basis to eligible entities that submit a proposal for a project that
fosters volunteerism.
3.3. ELIGIBLE ENTITIES. —
Eligible entities for the VIP are:
(a) Non-profit organizations,
(b) For-profit businesses,
(c) Public and private educational institutions, including
elementary schools, secondary schools, and institutions
of higher education.
4. SEC. 4. USE OF FUNDS.

4.1. IN GENERAL. —
VIP funds may be used to:
(1) Create or expand volunteer opportunities,
(2) Develop and implement training for volunteers,
(3) Implement systems to track and recognize volunteer
service, and
(4) Provide benefits to volunteers, such as academic credit or
workplace incentives.
5. SEC. 5. MATCHING FUNDS.
An eligible entity that receives a VIP grant must provide
matching funds equal to at least 25 percent of the grant amount.
6. SEC. 6. REPORTING.
Each entity that receives a VIP grant shall report annually to the
Secretary on the use of the funds and the impact of the funded
project on volunteerism.

fiscal years 2023 through 2028 to carry out this Act.
