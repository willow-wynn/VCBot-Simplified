Mr. Anka of Lincoln (for himself) introduced the following bill
A BILL
To combine the Red River Gorge Geological Area with Daniel Boone National Forest into
one National Park, provide necessary funding for upkeep, staffing, and establish an
ecological and environmental education program supervised by local universities.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of
America in Congress assembled,
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
SHORT TITLE— “Red River Gorge and Daniel Boone National Park Act of
2024”
SEC. 2. DEFINITIONS.
(a) DEFINITIONS—For the purposes of this Act:
1. "National Park" refers to the combined Red River Gorge Geological Area and Daniel
Boone National Forest.
2. "Secretary" refers to the Secretary of the Interior.
3. "Local Universities" refers to universities located in the state of Kentucky that will
voluntarily supervise the ecological and environmental education program

SEC. 3. ESTABLISHMENT OF RED RIVER GORGE AND DANIEL
BOONE NATIONAL PARK.
(a) ESTABLISHMENT—-There is established the Red River Gorge and Daniel
Boone National Park (referred to in this Act as the “National Park”) as a unit of the
National Park System..
(b) BOUNDARIES—The boundaries of the National Park shall encompass all areas
currently designated as the Red River Gorge Geological Area and the Daniel Boone
National Forest.
SEC. 4. ADMINISTRATION.
(a) MANAGEMENT—-The National Park shall be administered by the Secretary of
the Interior in accordance with this Act and the laws generally applicable to units of
the National Park System.
(b) FUNDING—There are authorized to be appropriated such sums as are necessary
for the management and operation of the National Park, including maintenance,
staffing, and educational programs.
SEC. 5. STAFFING AND UPKEEP.
(a) STAFFING—The Secretary shall ensure that the National Park is adequately
staffed with qualified personnel to manage and protect its resources.
(b) UPKEEP—The Secretary shall ensure that the National Park is maintained in a
manner that preserves its natural, cultural, and historical resources.
SEC. 6. ECOLOGICAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL EDUCATION
PROGRAM.
(a) ESTABLISHMENT–The Secretary shall establish an ecological and
environmental education program within the National Park.
(b) SUPERVISION–The program shall be supervised by local universities, in
coordination with the National Park Service, to promote research, education, and
public awareness about the park’s ecological and environmental significance.
(c) FUNDING–There are authorized to be appropriated such sums as are necessary
to implement and sustain the ecological and environmental education program.

SEC. 7. COLLABORATION WITH LOCAL UNIVERSITIES.
(a) AGREEMENTS: The Secretary shall enter into cooperative agreements with
local universities to develop and implement the ecological and environmental
education program.
(b) RESEARCH AND EDUCATION: The agreements shall include provisions for
research opportunities, educational activities, and community outreach programs
that leverage the expertise and resources of the universities.
SEC. 8. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS.
(a) ANNUAL REPORT–The Secretary shall submit an annual report to Congress
detailing the status of the National Park, including management activities, staffing
levels, maintenance needs, and the progress of the ecological and environmental
education program.
(b) PUBLIC AVAILABILITY–The report shall be made available to the public
through the National Park Service website.
SEC. 9. EFFECTIVE DATE.
(a) EFFECTIVE DATE–This Act shall take effect on the date of its enactment.
