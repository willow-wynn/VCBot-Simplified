SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
This Act may be cited as the “Claudette Colvin Commemorative Coin and Postal
Services Act”.
SEC. 2. FINDINGS.
(a) The Congress finds the following:
(i) Born in Montgomery, Alabama, Claudette Colvin, age 15 refused to move to
give up her seat to a white male — nine months before, American Activist
Rosa Louise McCauley Parks;
(ii) Colvin was forcibly removed from the bus and arrested for disturbing the
peace, assault of a police officer, and disobeying segregation laws; and
eventually sentenced to indefinite probation for assault of a police officer;
(iii) Colvin was not recognized nationally at the time for her historic protest of
Jim Crow segregation laws due to her age, appearance, and pregnancy;

(iv) Diversity is one of the greatest strengths of the United States, and the motto
inscribed on the Great Seal of the United States, “E pluribus unum”, Latin
for “out of many, one”, symbolizes that individuals in the United States from
all walks of life are unified by shared values;
(v) November 13, 1956, the Supreme Court upheld the decision of the District
Court that the State of Alabama’s bus segregation laws violated the rights of
African Americans as stated in the Fourteenth Amendment to the
Constitution;
(vi) We must recognize the understanding of the history of the United States and
the first principles of the United States is indispensable to the survival of the
United States as a free people;
(vii) the Senate and the House of Representatives, of the United States; in Congress
Assembled, recognizes Claudette Colvin as an integral figure of the civil
rights movement, for which she, as a teenager, advocated for the
constitutional rights of all African Americans;
SEC. 3. COIN SPECIFICATIONS.
(a) Denominations.—The Secretary of the Treasury (hereafter in this Act referred to as
the “Secretary”) shall mint and issue the following coin:
(i) $5 GOLD COINS.—Not more than 50,000 $5 coins, which shall—
(1) weigh 8.359 grams;
(2) have a diameter of 0.850 inches; and
(3) contain not less than 90 percent gold.
(ii) (2) $1 SILVER COINS.—Not more than 400,000 $1 coins, which shall—
(1) weigh 26.73 grams;
(2) have a diameter of 1.500 inches; and
(3) contain not less than 90 percent silver.
(iii) HALF-DOLLAR CLAD COINS.—Not more than 750,000 half-dollar coins
which shall—
(1) weigh 11.34 grams;
(2) have a diameter of 1.205 inches; and

(3) be mintedto thespecificationsfor half-dollar coins containedinsection
5112(b) of title31,UnitedStatesCode.
(b) Thecoins mintedunder thisAct shallbelegaltender, asprovidedinsection 5103oftitle
31,UnitedStatesCode.
(c) Numismatic Items.—For purposesofsections 5134and5136of title31, UnitedStates
Code, allcoins mintedunder this Actshallbeconsidered tobe numismaticitems.
SEC. 4. DESIGN OF COINS.
(a) Design Requirements.—
(i) IN GENERAL.—The designs of the coins minted under this Act shall be
emblematic of Claudette Colvin and their impact on the development of the
United States and Alabama State.
(ii) DESIGNATION AND INSCRIPTIONS.—On each coin minted under this Act,
there shall be—
(1) a designation of the value of the coin;
(2) the inscription “1939–”; and
(3) inscriptions of the words “Liberty”, “In God We Trust”, “United States
of America”, and “E Pluribus Unum”.
SEC. 5. ISSUANCE OF COINS.
(a) Quality Of Coins.—Coins minted under this Act shall be issued in uncirculated and
proof qualities.
(b) Period For Issuance.—The Secretary may issue coins minted under this Act only
during the 1-year period beginning on January 1, 2025.
SEC. 6. SALE OF COINS.
(a) Sale Price.—The coins issued under this Act shall be sold by the Secretary at a price
equal to the sum of—
(i) the face value of the coins;
(ii) the surcharge provided in section 7(a) with respect to such coins; and
(iii) the cost of designing and issuing the coins (including labor, materials, dies,
use of machinery, overhead expenses, marketing, and shipping).

(b) Bulk Sales.—The Secretary shall make bulk sales of the coins issued under this Act
at a reasonable discount.
(c) Prepaid Orders.—
(i) IN GENERAL.—The Secretary shall accept prepaid orders for the coins
minted under this Act before the issuance of such coins.
(ii) DISCOUNT.—Sale prices with respect to prepaid orders under paragraph (1)
shall be at a reasonable discount.
SEC. 7. CLAUDETTE COLVIN POST OFFICE BUILDING.
(a) Designation.—The facility of the United States Postal Service located at 6701
Winton Blount Blvd, Montgomery, AL 36119 shall be known and designated as the
“Claudette Colvin Post Office Building”.
(b) References.—Any reference in a law, map, regulation, document, paper, or other
record of the United States to the facility referred to in subsection (a) shall be
deemed to be a reference to the “Claudette Colvin Post Office Building”.
