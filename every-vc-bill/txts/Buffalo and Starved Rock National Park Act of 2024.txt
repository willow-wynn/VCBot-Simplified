Mr. Anka of Lincoln (for himself, Mr. Nord of Pacifica) introduced the
following bill
A BILL
To designate and provide the necessary funding for Buffalo Rock and Starved Rock State
Park in Illinois as one National Park, called the Buffalo and Starved Rock National Park,
and to provide funding for local universities to partner with the park for research initiatives
and college credit programs.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of
America in Congress assembled,
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
SHORT TITLE—This Act may be cited as the "Buffalo and Starved Rock
National Park Act."
SEC. 2. FINDINGS
(a) FINDINGS—Congress finds the following:
i. Illinois currently has no national parks, despite its rich natural resources and
diverse landscapes.
ii. Designating Buffalo Rock and Starved Rock State Parks as a national park
will enhance conservation efforts, protect biodiversity, and preserve the
cultural and historical significance of the area.
iii. Establishing Buffalo and Starved Rock National Park will promote tourism,
boost the local economy, and provide recreational opportunities for residents
and visitors.

iv. The partnership between the national park and local universities will foster
research, education, and workforce development, benefiting both students
and the broader community.
SEC. 3. DESIGNATION OF BUFFALO AND STARVED ROCK NATIONAL
PARK
(a) DESIGNATION—-The Buffalo Rock State Park and Starved Rock State Park in
Illinois are hereby designated as Buffalo and Starved Rock National Park (hereafter
referred to as the "National Park").
(b) ADMINISTRATION—The Secretary of the Interior (hereafter referred to as the
"Secretary"), acting through the National Park Service, shall administer the
National Park in accordance with this Act and the laws generally applicable to units
of the National Park System.
SEC. 4. FUNDING
(a) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS—-.—There are authorized to be
appropriated such sums as may be necessary to carry out this Act, including—
i. The development and maintenance of park facilities and infrastructure;
ii. The implementation of conservation and preservation programs;
iii. The promotion of tourism and educational activities.
(b) LOCAL PARTNERSHIPS—The Secretary is authorized to provide grants and
enter into cooperative agreements with local governments, nonprofit organizations,
and other entities to advance the purposes of this Act.
SEC. 5. UNIVERSITY PARTNERSHIPS
(a) RESEARCH INITIATIVES—The Secretary shall establish a program to facilitate
partnerships between the National Park and local universities for research
initiatives related to ecology, conservation, geology, and other relevant fields.
(b) COLLEGE CREDIT PROGRAMS—The Secretary shall collaborate with local
universities to develop and implement programs that allow students to earn college
credit through internships, research projects, and other educational activities
conducted at the National Park.
(c) FUNDING FOR EDUCATIONAL PROGRAMS–There are authorized to be
appropriated such sums as may be necessary to support the research initiatives and
college credit programs described in this section.

SEC. 6. EFFECTIVE DATE
EFFECTIVE DATE—This Act shall take effect on the date of its enactment.
SEC. 7. SEVERABILITY
SEVERABILITY—If any provision of this Act, or the application thereof, is held
invalid, the remainder of this Act and the application of such provision to other
persons or circumstances shall not be affected thereby.
SEC. 8. REGULATIONS
REGULATIONS—The Secretary may promulgate such regulations as are necessary
to carry out this Act.
SEC. 8. REPORT TO CONGRESS
REPORTING—The Secretary shall submit a report to Congress within three years
of the enactment of this Act on the status of the National Park, including the
implementation of university partnerships and the impact on local conservation and
tourism efforts..
