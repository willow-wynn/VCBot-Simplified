IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED STATES
JANUARY 9, 2021
Mr. MILLER (for himself, Mr. PLURIBUS, Ms. KYOUKO, Mr.
LIGHTWOOD, Ms. TOWARD, Mr. WHITE, Mr. BOBAYYY)
introduced the following bill;
A BILL
To dedicate the post office located at 240 Peachtree Street NW Bsmt, Atlanta, GA 30303 as the
John Lewis Post Office, in an effort to dedicate the work of Congressman John Lewis.
Be it enacted by the Senate and the House of Representatives of the United States
of America in Congress Assembled,
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
This Act may be cited as the “John Lewis Post Office Act”
SEC. 2. FINDINGS.
Congress finds that—
(a) John Lewis was a man that fought for civil rights for African Americans.
(b) John Lewis was arrested 40 times through the 1900’s fighting for civil rights.
(c) John Lewis was nearly killed multiple times, most notably on “Bloody Sunday” where
people crossed the Edmund Pettus Bridge in Alabama and were met with brutal force.
(d) John Lewis was one of the last surviving of the “Big Six”, who were the main leaders of
the Civil Rights movement.
SEC. 3. JOHN LEWIS POST OFFICE.

(a) DESIGNATION.— The facility of the United States Postal Service located at 240
Peachtree Street NW Bsmt, Atlanta, GA 30303, shall be known and designated as
the “John Lewis Post Office”.
(b) REFERENCES.— Any reference in a law, map, regulation, document, paper, or
other records of the United States to the facility referred to in subsection (a) shall
be deemed to be a reference to the “John Lewis Post Office”.
SEC. 4. ENACTMENT.
EFFECTIVE DATE.— The provisions of this Act shall come into force immediately upon
passage.
