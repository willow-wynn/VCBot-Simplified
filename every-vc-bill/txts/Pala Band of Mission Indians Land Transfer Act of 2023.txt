AN ACT
To take certain land located in San Diego County, region of California, Pacifica, into trust
for the benefit of the Pala Band of Mission Indians.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House Representative of the United States of America in
Congress assembled,
SECTION 1: SHORT TITLE
This Act may be cited as the “Pala Band of Mission Indians Land Transfer Act of
2023”.
SECTION 2: TRANSFER OF LAND IN TRUST FOR THE PALA BAND OF MISSION
INDIANS
TRANSFER AND ADMINISTRATION. —
1. TRANSFER OF LANDS INTO TRUST. — If, not later than 30 days after the
date of this Act, the Tribe transfers title to the land referred to in the
subsection (b) to the United States, the Secretary, not later than 60 days after
such transfer, shall take that land into trust for the benefit of the tribe.
2. ADMINISTRATION. — The land transferred under paragraph (1) shall be
part of the Pala Indian Reservation and administered under accordance with
the laws and regulations generally applicable to land held in trust by the
United States for an Indian Tribe.
LAND DESCRIPTION. — The land referred to in subsection (a) (1) is the
approximately 712.12 acres of land located in San Diego County, region of California,

RULES OF CONSTRUCTION. —Nothing in this Act shall—
a. Enlarge, impair, or otherwise affect any right or claim of the Tribe to
any land or interest in land that is in existence before the date of the
enactment of this Act.
b. Affect any water right of the Tribe in existence before the ate of the
enactment of this Act; or
c. Terminate or limit any access in any way to any right-of-way or
right-of-use issue, granted, or permitted before the date of the
enactment of this Act.
